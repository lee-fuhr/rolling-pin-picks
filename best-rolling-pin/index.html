A good rolling pin is a humble workhorse: it flattens dough evenly, doesn’t stick or warp, and feels comfortable in your hands. Whether you’re making pie crusts, pasta, or cookies, the right pin makes the job smoother and more consistent. Most rolling pins fall into two camps: tapered French-style pins, which give you fine pressure control, and straight pins with handles, which many home bakers find easier to grip. Materials matter too, hardwood like maple or beech is durable and ages well, while marble adds weight and stays cool for sensitive doughs. Your choice should match your baking habits: how often you roll, what types of dough you tackle, and your storage space.
